Stewardship

When we give of our time, talent and treasure, as a response to the generosity of God, we call it Stewardship. Our capacity to give is only because God has first given to us.

When we give of our time, talent and treasure to our parish, Saint James The Less, or other charities, we acknowledge that we are not the starting point for the gift, but we are simply passing on a portion of what we have received.

Borrowed from the Diocese of Charleston, we can learn more from the video below.
Stewardship of Time.

The stewardship of time is the careful, wise, and Godly use of the time God gives us.

Our time is a very precious gift given to us by God, but yet it can't be stored up like grain in a barn. Most of us feel we don't have enough time for all the things we want to do. Everyone is given the same amount of time; 24 hours each day, 168 hours each week. Once a moment passes, it is gone forever.

So the question becomes: How do we choose to use our time?

Described in Colossians 4-5,"our task is to make the most of our time by using it wisely."

Ephesians 5:16 further states that "this includes spending time with God and His church, as well as time with family, friends, and neighborsâ€”being there for them and pointing them to Jesus."


"For Catholics, stewardship of time should include setting aside periods for family prayer, for the reading of Scripture, for visits to the Blessed Sacrament, and for the attendance at Mass during the week..."
-U.S. Bishops' Pastoral Letter on Stewardship.


The Godly Use of Time.

There are many ways of giving of our time in stewardship. Some of them are as follows:
  • Receiving the Eucharist each week at Mass, we enjoy a unique union with Christ and, in him, with one another. Here His love, indeed His very self, flows into His disciples and, through them and their practice of stewardship, to the entire human race.

  • Through daily prayer, we develop a closer personal relationship with God, sharing with Him our successes and failures, and asking for His guidance in our daily lives.

  • Reading Scripture daily helps us reflect on God's word. It also tells of God's immense love for us.

  • Assisting individuals, organizations, and the Church is another way to share our personal time for the glory of God.




Stewardship of Talent.

All of us have been blessed with special talents and gifts from God, whether we are aware of them or not or whether we have developed our talents or not. These talents and gifts are not given to us just for our own use, but are given to us for the enrichment of the lives of others in order for us to serve God better.


Christ asks of us to do His works in our community and his Church. We need to rejuvenate ourselves through service. Reaching out with love to others, while sometimes difficult, is nonetheless spiritually enriching. It is a necessary part of Catholic life. Some of the ways that you can share your talent here at Saint James are as follows:
  • Serve in church as a minister
  • Sing in the choir
  • Visit the sick and elderly
  • Teach those who want to learn
  • Help in tasks in the office
  • Console the lonely and sorrowful
The tasks above are organized by the different ministries here at Saint James. Besides serving our Church and our community, the ministries foster bonds of friendships in the process of deepening each minister's personal relationship with God.

Come join us! Share your God-given talents!

Let us together we join in strengthening the Church and the mission that was entrusted to us, His people.

Stewardship of Treasure

Honor the Lord with your wealth, with first fruits of all your produce; Then will your barns be filled with plenty, with new wine your vats will overflow.
Proverbs 3:9-10
Throughout Scripture, we are taught to give God our "first fruits" or the "choicest portion". We believe that it is this teaching of Christ on the stewardship of treasure that makes it possible for us to be remarkably supported by our parishioners' generous offerings through the weekly collections.

Just like any other church, we rely on parishioner financial support for our operational costs: for maintenance of the physical buildings and other facilities, for our utilities, for our administrative costs, and for costs for ministry and outreach, among others.

We couldn't thank you enough for your continued commitment and support to the Church's mission. In return, it is in prudence that we pledge our commitment to take seriously our duty of care of the treasure you have shared.

We continually ask that you prayerfully examine your personal finances for an opportunity to lend support.


Please call the parish office for information on how you can financially support Saint James the Less Church.


We thank you in advance.
