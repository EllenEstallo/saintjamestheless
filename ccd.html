Religious Education of Children
(CCD)

"Catechesis aims to bring about in the believer
an ever more mature faith in Jesus Christ,
a deeper knowledge and love of his message,
and a firm commitment to follow him.”
The Religious Education of Children, or CCD, is central to the parish life of Saint James The Less Church. We acknowledge the exhortation of Vatican II, recognizing the parents, as primarily and principally responsible for the education of their children. It is in this regard that we are committed to enhancing what our dear parents have already provided by ensuring that your children receive the proper formation to receive the Sacraments in a timely manner.
Throughout the year, our program fosters an intentionally inclusive community of children, parents, and catechists, built on purposeful knowledge and Christian values. We strive to model God’s love and grace for our children grow in dignity, respect and compassion, that they learn to use faith and reason to explore lifelong questions of purpose, meaning, and love.

In addition, we encourage our children to know and develop a personal and intimate relationship with Christ, Our Lord and Savior, along with a commitment of love and service to His Church. It is our belief that a special personal bond with Jesus is essential to a lifelong process toward maturity in Christian Faith as a lived reality.
Our volunteer catechists are dedicated to their faith, to our parish families, and especially to our children. They are specifically trained by our Diocese to teach the doctrine of the Roman Catholic Church. With a value-based approach to learning, they will set formative foundations for our children's spiritual growth to help them apply faith and reason to their daily lives.
Please consider enrolling your children in our Religious Education Program (CCD).
Contact us for more information. We look forward to hearing from you!
